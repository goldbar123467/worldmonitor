Step 1 · Your Mission

You have been recruited as a Global Issues Investigator. Newsrooms, classrooms, and community groups need clear, trustworthy explanations of what is happening in the world — and why it matters. Your job is to choose a real-world issue, dig into credible sources, separate facts from opinions, and turn what you learn into a one-page Canva infographic your peers will actually read.

⏱ Estimated time: 45–60 minutes

Learning objectives

  • Choose and frame a current global issue.
  • Evaluate source credibility using the CRAAP test.
  • Distinguish facts from opinions.
  • Capture research notes using the 5 Ws + How.
  • Synthesize three key insights for a public audience.
  • Translate findings into a clear Canva infographic.

Step 3 · Source Sleuth

Great investigators check their sources. Use the CRAAP test as your detective lens:

  • Currency — How recent is the information?
  • Relevance — Does it actually answer your question?
  • Authority — Who wrote it? What are their credentials?
  • Accuracy — Is evidence cited? Can you verify it?
  • Purpose — Inform, persuade, sell, or entertain?

Step 4 · Fact vs. Opinion

Reporters share facts (verifiable). Commentators share opinions (a viewpoint). Read each statement and decide which it is.

  • "In 2023, global average surface temperatures were the warmest on record, according to NASA."

  • "Electric cars are clearly the best solution to climate change."

  • "The World Health Organization reports more than 1 billion people live with a mental health disorder."

  • "Schools should ban smartphones because they are ruining kids' attention spans."

  • "UNESCO data shows about 250 million children worldwide are out of school."

  • "AI will probably do more harm than good in the next ten years."

Step 7 · Build It in Canva

Time to turn your research into a one-page infographic your peers will actually stop and read.

Project brief

Audience: your peers and your local community.

Format: 1-page infographic — portrait, 1080 × 1350 px (or use the Canva "Infographic" preset).

Required elements

Design tips

  • Contrast: dark text on light backgrounds (or vice versa).
  • Hierarchy: the most important thing should be the biggest.
  • White space: leave room to breathe — don't fill every pixel.
  • One accent color for emphasis; keep the rest neutral.
  • Sans-serif body fonts at 14 pt or larger for readability.

For teachers

Time estimate

45–60 minutes for the on-screen activity, plus 30–45 minutes in Canva. Works well as a 2-day mini-unit or a single 90-minute block.

Differentiation

  • Scaffolded: assign topics, provide a curated source list, and pair students for Steps 3–5.
  • On-level: students choose their own topic and find their own credible sources.
  • Extension: require a primary source (interview, survey) and a counter-argument call-out.
  • EL / IEP: allow voice-to-text in textareas; provide sentence frames for the 3 Insights.

Standards alignment

  • ISTE Student Standards: 3 Knowledge Constructor (3a, 3b, 3d) and 6 Creative Communicator (6a, 6b, 6d).
  • CASEL competencies: Social Awareness (perspective-taking, global citizenship) and Responsible Decision-Making (analyzing situations, evaluating sources).
  • CCSS ELA: RI.7-12.7, RI.7-12.8, W.7-12.7, W.7-12.8 (research, source evaluation, integration of information).
